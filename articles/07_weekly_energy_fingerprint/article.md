Electricity demand has a daily rhythm. Work, school, cooking, heating and rest create patterns that household data can reveal with surprising clarity.

## A weekly fingerprint

Weekday and weekend use differ because routines differ. A heatmap turns those shifts into a readable weekly fingerprint: when demand rises, when it falls and how daily life shapes the grid.

## Why this matters for renewables

More wind and solar means more clean energy at particular times. Flexible demand—through smart appliances, storage, electric vehicles and well-designed tariffs—can help use that energy when it is abundant. Consumers should not be treated as a problem to manage; they can be participants in a better system when flexibility is simple and worthwhile.
