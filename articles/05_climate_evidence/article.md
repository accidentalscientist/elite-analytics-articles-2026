Climate evidence is strongest when independent records agree. Australian power-generation data describes the system that produces emissions. Atmospheric carbon-dioxide observations track the accumulating forcing. Surface and satellite temperature records show the resulting warming signal across different periods and measurement systems.

## One problem, several lenses

The long-run surface record shows a persistent shift in temperature anomalies. Satellite observations independently confirm recent warming and seasonal change. Cape Grim carbon-dioxide data shows the continuing upward pressure in the atmosphere. These are not interchangeable datasets, which is precisely why their agreement is valuable.

## Energy is the response lever

The evidence is not an abstract argument about charts. Electricity generation is one of the systems that can change the trajectory. The Australian power analysis therefore belongs beside the climate records: it connects a measurable problem to the infrastructure choices that can reduce emissions.

## What a serious response requires

The right response is neither panic nor complacency. It is disciplined system change: cleaner generation, transmission, storage, efficient demand and transparent measurement of progress.
