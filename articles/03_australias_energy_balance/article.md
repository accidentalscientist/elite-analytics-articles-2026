Australia's electricity transition matters enormously, but electricity is not the whole energy system. A national energy balance includes how energy is consumed, where it is supplied, how states differ and how deeply transport and industry still depend on petroleum.

## The wider system

The June energy series moves from national overview to detailed tables, state comparisons, usage patterns and petroleum statistics. Taken together, those views make a simple point: decarbonisation is not only a question of adding renewable electricity. It is also a question of changing the fuels that power mobility, industry and daily consumption.

## Why petroleum deserves its own lens

Oil-derived fuels are easy to overlook when electricity dominates public debate. Yet they remain embedded in transport, freight and many industrial activities. Electrification can reduce that dependence, but it also increases the importance of a reliable, affordable clean grid. The electricity transition and the petroleum transition are therefore connected, not competing agendas.

## A practical transition test

The useful test is whether Australia can reduce fossil-fuel dependence while maintaining the services people and businesses rely on. That means tracking supply, demand and end uses together. It also means being clear about where electricity can substitute for fossil fuels and where other solutions will be needed.
