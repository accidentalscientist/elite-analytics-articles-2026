Fifteen years of Premier League data can tell a story about more than champions. It can show how dominance emerges, how fair comparison changes the table and which performance traits are associated with sustained success.

## Start with trustworthy history

The series begins with data construction and quality checks. That work matters: an incorrect season assignment can produce an attractive but false historical story. The finished article should retain this lesson briefly, then foreground the corrected analysis.

## Beyond the league table

Raw totals reward longevity and volume. Per-game metrics make comparisons fairer across uneven sample sizes. Alternative rankings reveal whether teams create chances efficiently, defend consistently or rely on a narrow strength.

## Controlled aggression

The final modelling work adds a compelling question: can discipline and aggression be balanced in a way that supports elite performance? The answer is not a deterministic rule, but a useful example of how feature engineering and transparent modelling can turn football data into strategic insight.
